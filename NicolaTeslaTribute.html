Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla, a Serbian-American inventor, was best known for his contribution to the design of the modern alternating current electricity supply system.

Timeline of Nikola Tesla's Life

  • 1859: Nikola Tesla is born. Born to a Serbian Family in Smiljan, Tesla was nicknamed `Child of the Storm`, by his aunt.
  • 1870: Tesla's brush with death. Tesla moves to Karlovac to complete high school, 3 years later, he went back to Smiljan and shortly after his arrival he contracted Cholera. He was in the hospital for close to one year and faced near-death circumstances.
  • 1875: Tesla enrolls in college. He enrolled in a Polytechnic-based college on a military scholarship and achieved the highest grades possible.
  • 1880: Tesla moves to Prague to study Philosophy. He took up classes in Philosophy at a university, but unfortunately did not receive grades for the courses.
  • 1881: Tesla begins his career as Chief Electrical Engineer. He moved to Budapest to work for a telegraph company. During his time working there, he introduced several operation upswings which ultimately improved the performance of the company.
  • 1882: Tesla goes to work for Edison. Tesla moved to New York and started a manufacturing divison to produce dyanmos and motors.
  • 1885: Tesla submits his first patents. An attorney, Lemeul Serrel, helped in the submission of various patents. They financed a new light production company and Tesla developed an enhanced DC motor.
  • 1887: Tesla re-imagines the Electric Motor. Tesla came up with an induction motor that was operated by alternating current, a form of power that was becoming common in Europe and USA -- providing various benefits, especially in long-distance power transmission.
  • 1890: Tesla begins his work on wireless power transmission. Tesla spent over a decade trying to come up with new solutions for lighting. After 1980, he researched various approaches to transmitting power by inductive and capactive coupling.
  • 1891: Tesla patents the Tesla Coil. By the age of 35, he became a citizen of the US. In the same year, he also patended his famous Tesla coil.
  • 1943: Tesla's death. By age 86, Tesla died in a hotel room in New York. A medical professional determined the cause of his death and came to the conclusion that it was due to coronary complications.
